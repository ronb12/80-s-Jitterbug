Comparison against typical photo booth rental websites (SnapJoy, Feature Booth, PhotoG Booth, Puddles, MIA Photo Booth, etc.) and best-practice guides.

## Gaps vs Competitors (Recommended Additions)

### High impact (conversion & trust)

1. **FAQ page**  
   Competitors almost all have one. Include:
   - Setup/teardown time (e.g. 60–90 min setup)
   - Space needed (e.g. 7×9 ft minimum, 10×10 ft ideal)
   - Power (one outlet?)
   - Deposit and when balance is due (e.g. 50% to book, rest 30 days before)
   - Minimum hours (e.g. 3 hours)
   - What’s included (unlimited photos, props, attendant where applicable)
   - Custom branding (logos, overlays) — yes/no and how

2. **Service area / location**  
   People search “photo booth rental [city].” Add:
   - Cities/regions you serve
   - Travel radius or “travel fee beyond X miles”
   - Improves local SEO and sets expectations

3. **Real photos**  
   Right now: hero and gallery use placeholders (gradients/emojis). Competitors use real booth + event photos.  
   - Add 6–12 real photos to Gallery (your booth, backdrops, guests)  
   - One strong hero image or short video of the booth in action  
   Even a few real shots will significantly improve trust and conversions.

4. **Deposit & payment**  
   Not mentioned anywhere. Add to Packages or FAQ (and optionally to booking confirmation):
   - Deposit to secure (e.g. 50%)
   - When final payment is due
   - Accepted methods (e.g. Venmo, card, check)

### Medium impact (credibility & SEO)

5. **Stronger testimonials**  
   You have quotes; competitors often add:
   - Photo of the couple/guest (with permission)
   - Full name or “Sarah M.” + event type
   - Optional: link to Google Reviews or a review snippet

6. **Local SEO**  
   - Use your real city/region in title or description (e.g. “Photo Booth Rentals in [City]”).
   - One short “Serving [City] and the [Region]” line on Home or About.
   - Helps for “photo booth rental [city]” and “wedding photo booth [city].”

7. **Add-ons**  
   Many sites list add-ons: extra hour, extra prints, custom backdrop, logo on strips.  
   - Add a small “Add-ons” section on Packages or a short list with prices.  
   Increases perceived value and average order size.

### Nice to have

8. **Backdrops**  
   Some companies have a “Backdrops” page with options. Optional; can be folded into Gallery or Packages.

9. **Privacy & Terms**  
   Footer links to Privacy Policy and Terms of Service (even short versions) improve trust and can be required if you collect data or payments.

10. **Contact details**  
    Replace placeholder email/phone with real business contact (or clearly mark “Example” until you go live).

---

## Summary

- **Structure and flows:** You’re in good shape — core pages, booking, admin, and branding are in place and comparable to competitors.
- **Content and trust:** The main gaps are **FAQ**, **service area**, **real photos**, and **deposit/payment**. Adding these will bring you in line with typical photo booth rental sites and support conversions.
- **SEO:** Add **location** (city/region) in copy and meta so you can compete for “photo booth rental [city]” and “wedding photo booth [city].”

**Suggested order:**  
1) FAQ page, 2) Service area on Home/About, 3) Deposit/payment in FAQ or Packages, 4) Replace gallery (and ideally hero) with real photos, 5) Local SEO and add-ons.
